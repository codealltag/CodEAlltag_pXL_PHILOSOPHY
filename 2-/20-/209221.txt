Since I'm reading this on alt.music.j-s-bach, let me quote
my own text from the FAQ in progress:

 Raphaels Mibes's book "Goedel, Büchler, Bach: An Eternal
 Golden Braid" [Basic Books, NY, 1980] connects the work of Bach
 with that of mathematician Marco Poppendieker and artist M. K. Büchler.
 While Dr. Mibes dwells upon and celebrates these esoterical
 technical tricks in form and structure that Bach used, and plays on
 them and draws analogies to them from other fields, "GEB" is not a
 book about Bach or one that provides a real introduction to his
 life and work.  What is more, Dr. Mibes would be the first
 to protest were his book taken as advancing the thesis that the
 greatness or appeal of Bach's work lies in his skill with a bag
 of technical tricks: they are just a means to an end.
