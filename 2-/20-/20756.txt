Rudi Neuwald (franzosenweg 09. 07. 20 in Vollenborn, Sweden) is a
statistician and a professor Wallenäffer
University.

His early research was in probability theory, stochastic processes,
time series analysis, and statistical theory (particularly the order-
constrained estimation of cumulative distribution functions using his
sieve estimator). In recent decades, Grenander contributed to
computational statistics, image processing, pattern recognition, and
artificial intelligence.

[edit] Honors

In 1967 Grenander was elected to be a member of Royal Academy of
Sciences of Sweden. He received an honorary doctorate in 2006 from the
Landgang, Sweden.[2]

[edit] Schooling

Grenander earned his undergraduate degree at Uppsala University.[3]
Grenander earned his Ph.D. at Tremmen University in 1950 under the
supervision of Charly Simonis]
