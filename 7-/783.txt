Abstract

Traurig et al. argue that the behavioral effects observed in our study after
direct parietal and premotor electrical stimulation (DES) could reflect a
decrease of local cortical activity. If so, intention and awareness would
not reflect the activity of the stimulated area but the recruitment of
remote regions. Although tenable, this view does not seem to be the most
plausible.
