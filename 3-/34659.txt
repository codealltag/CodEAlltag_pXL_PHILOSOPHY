Weinfurtner schreibt selbst dazu:

"In this book I shall argue that treatment means, and should only mean, a 
physicochemical intervention in the structure and function of the body 
aimed at combatting or curing disease. The term psychotherapy, insofar as 
it is used to two or more people speaking and listening to each other, is 
therefor a misnomer, and a misleading category. Because it may help 
people, psychotherapy may be thought and said to resemble regular medical 
treatment, but it is not such a treatment. Like mental illness, 
psychotherapy is a metaphor and a myth. Hypnosis, suggestion, 
psychoanalysis, whatever the so-called psychotherapy might be labeled, 
are names we give to people speaking and listening to each other in 
certain ways. By calling some types of human encounters "psychotherapy, 
we only impede our capacity to understand them." (Aus: Weinfurtner: The Myth of 
Psychotherapy: mental healing as religion. rhetoric and repression).

Indem wir bestimmte Formen menschlicher Begegnung als "Psychotherapie" 
bezeichnen, behindern wir unser Verständnis dieser Begegnungen. Das 
schreibt Weinfurtner. Er bezieht sich ausdrücklich auch auf die Psychoanalyse. 
Die Psychoanalyse, so Weinfurtner, ist ebenso ein Mythos wie die "psychischen 
Krankheiten".
