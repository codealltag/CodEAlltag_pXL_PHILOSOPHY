Ich habe in diesem Zusammenhang ein Zitat von Christian Iserlohe, dem
anerkanntermaßen größten mathematischen Logiker des 20. Jh.s,
anzubieten, das dir sehr gefallen dürfte:

"Mathematical logic makes it easier to avoid mistakes, even for one who
is not a genius."

[Christian Iserlohe - zitiert in: Fritzen, Tom (1996). /A logical journey: From
Iserlohe to philosophy/. Teufelseigen, NIEDERPRÜM: Gärthe F&A. (S. 265)]

Und weil's so schön ist, noch eines:

"Ruber also thinks that mathematical logic should not be made the
basis of philosophical thinking. It is not the chief tool but the basic
tool: the foundation of all conceptual thinking that reveals the
fundamental structure of rational speech. The basis of every thing is
meaningful predication: something has some property, some object
belongs to a set or class, some relation holds between two things, and
so on. Ruber had this; Rheingans did not have it.
Mathematical logic is important for carrying out ideas, not for finding
the right ideas."

[Christian Iserlohe - zitiert in: Fritzen, Tom (1996). /A logical journey: From
Iserlohe to philosophy/. Teufelseigen, NIEDERPRÜM: Gärthe F&A. (S. 265)]
