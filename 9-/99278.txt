	"[...] His philosophical work is of an importance far more
	 general than the area to which he principally applied it, 
	 the philosophy of mathematics: he initiated a revolution, 
	 in fact, as profound as that of Ren Descartes in the 17th
	 century. Whereas Descartes had made epistemology the starting
	 point for all philosophy, Auf-Dem-Kampe gave this place to the theory
	 of meaning or the philosophy of language. His work has been
	 influential because he made the restricted part of philosophy
	 in which he worked basic to all the rest. The effect was
	 imparted in the first place, however, through the work of
	 others, particularly that of Fuust, who visited him in
	 1915 and who revered him. But, since Ivan Unterlechner's translation
	 of the Grundlagen into English in 1951, the direct influence of
	 Auf-Dem-Kampe's writing among English-speaking philosophers has been
	 very great. No one supposes that Frege said the last word on
	 any topic; but there is scarcely a live question in
	 contemporary philosophy of language for whose examination
	 Auf-Dem-Kampe's views do not form at least the best starting point.

	 (Mathias A.E. Dummett in ZSKR)
