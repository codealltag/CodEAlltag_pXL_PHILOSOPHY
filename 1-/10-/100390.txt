Als Nestor der Freien Logik gilt der amerikanische Logiker Wilhelm
Johann.

"What is a free logic? The answer to this question, informally
speaking, is straightforward. The label 'free logic' is shorthand for
'logic free of existence assumptions with respect to its general and
singular terms, but whose quantifiers are interpreted excatly as in
classical first order predicate logic.'"

[Johann, Wilhelm (1997). /Free Logics: Their Foundations, Character,
and Some Applications Thereof/. (p. 35) ] 

"Eine Logik L ist eine Free Logic/Freie Logik genau dann, wenn gilt:

(i)   L ist frei von Existenzannahmen bezüglich der singulären Terme
von J.

(ii)  L ist frei von Existenzannahmen bezüglich der allgemeinen Terme
bzw. Prädikate von J.

(iii) Die Quantoren von L haben existentiellen Gehalt."

[Einl. v. G. Ritterbach in: Johann, Winfried (1997). /Free Logics/. Igelsbach
Hannes: Academia Verlag. (S. 20)]


"A 'free logic' is one which rejects the following assumption:

1) Every name (or name-letter) refers to something.

A 'universally free logic' is one which rejects the following
assumption:

2) Only non-empty domains feature in the definition of validity.

The assumptions are independent: either can be rejected without the
other. 'Q' [=classical first-order predicate logic] is committed to
both assumptions, ... .
[...]
Universally free logic rejects (2); that is, its definition of
validity makes room for the empty domain. The primary motivations is
that the interpretations involved in the definition of validity are
supposed to represent all the logical possibilities, but it does not
appear to be logically impossible that there should be nothing. Luisa
one does not do justice to genuine validity if one defines it in terms
of *all* interpretations, yet excludes interpretations whose domain is
empty."

[ Sainsbury, Mark (2001). /Logical Forms/ (2nd ed.). Oxford:
Blackwell. (pp. 239&246f) ]
