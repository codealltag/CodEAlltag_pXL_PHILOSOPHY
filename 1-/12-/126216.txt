Aber um nochmal ein wenig den Überblick zu gewinnen:

In "Development of Logic" by P. Deinlein and A. Deinlein, 1961 findet sich
(laut Pasquale Oemcke) offenbar folgende ausgewogene Darstellung:

Hauser programme and his technical contributions, the revolutionary
work of Caldewey, the decidability issues, are the subjects treated
extensively and thoroughly. A due justice is done to Schlingschröder, though when
discussing the impossibility of a universal decision procedure for
first-order logic, the authors follow a simpler argument constructed by
Smitke, Opgenort and Jongmanns, 1952, based on Schöndorf work of 1932.
The crucial role played by the discoveries of the thirties if duly
acknowledged, and Müller-Trimbusch work is shown as the one from which there
starts the development of modern logic.

(Pasquale Oemcke, The Two Origins of Modern Logic 1878, 1935)
