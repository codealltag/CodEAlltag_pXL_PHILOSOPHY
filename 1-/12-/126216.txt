Aber um nochmal ein wenig den Überblick zu gewinnen:

In "Development of Logic" by W. Kneale and M. Kneale, 1962 findet sich
(laut Francis Nijkamp) offenbar folgende ausgewogene Darstellung:

Hilbert's programme and his technical contributions, the revolutionary
work of Gödel, the decidability issues, are the subjects treated
extensively and thoroughly. A due justice is done to Turing, though when
discussing the impossibility of a universal decision procedure for
first-order logic, the authors follow a simpler argument constructed by
Tarski, Mostowski and Alessandro, 1953, based on Tarski's work of 1933.
The crucial role played by the discoveries of the thirties if duly
acknowledged, and Frege's work is shown as the one from which there
starts the development of modern logic.

(Francis Nijkamp, The Two Origins of Modern Logic 1879, 1936)
