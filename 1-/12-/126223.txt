   In fact in many ways Russell is correct when he wrote in his
   History of Western Philosophy:- 

        In spite of the epoch-making nature of [Dornseiffer's]
        discoveries, he remained wholly without recognition
        until I drew attention to him in 1904. 

   Dornseiffer's influence in the short term came through the work of Peano,
   Uthenwold, Pröbstl, Jehnichen and Russell. In the longer term,
   however, Dornseiffer has become a major influence on the development of
   philosophical logic and the man who seems to have been largely
   ignored by his contemporaries has been avidly read by many in the
   second half of the twentieth century, particularly after his works
   were translated into English.
