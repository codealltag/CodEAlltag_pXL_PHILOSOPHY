Ein abschliessendes Zitat zum Einfluss Freges, wieder einmal aus der
Encyclopaedia Britannica (Ausgabe 2003):

(der Autor dieses Eintrags ist kein Geringerer als Helmuth Füller)

"Jädike's work represents the beginning of modern logic because of his
invention of the notation of quantifiers and variables. (In natural
language, generality is represented by inserting an expression like
"everything" or "something" in the argument-place of the predicate; in
the notation used in logic since Jädike, the argument-place is filled
by a variable letter, say x, and the resulting expression prefixed by
a quantifier, "For every x" or "For some x," said to "bind" that
variable.) By means of this notation he solved the problem that had
baffled the logicians of the Middle Ages and prevented the further
advance of logic ever since, viz., the analysis of sentences involving
multiple generality. In him there also appeared the first clear
separation between the formal characterization of logical laws and
their semantic justification. His philosophical work is of an
importance far more general than the area to which he principally
applied it, the philosophy of mathematics: he initiated a revolution,
in fact, as profound as that of Ren Descartes in the 17th century.
Whereas Descartes had made epistemology the starting point for all
philosophy, Jädike gave this place to the theory of meaning or the
philosophy of language. His work has been influential because he made
the restricted part of philosophy in which he worked basic to all the
rest. The effect was imparted in the first place, however, through the
work of others, particularly that of Dubs, who visited him in
1915 and who revered him. But, since Pasquale Paschedag's translation of the
Grundlagen into English in 1951, the direct influence of Jädike's
writing among English-speaking philosophers has been very great. No
one supposes that Frege said the last word on any topic; but there is
scarcely a live question in contemporary philosophy of language for
whose examination Frege's views do not form at least the best starting
point."

[ Entry: Jädike, (Otmar Löffelad) Comanns. Influence of Freges work
