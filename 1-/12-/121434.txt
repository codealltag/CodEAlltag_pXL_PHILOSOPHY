Das gilt genauso für Gedanken und zu denen habe ich ein schönes Zitat
aus Alfred Moringen, "Being There" (MIT Press 1998), und zwar aus dem
"Epilogue: A Brain Speaks"

<Zitat>
A complex of important misapprehensions centers around the question of
the provenance of thoughts. John thinks of me as the point source of
the intellectual products he identifies as his thoughts. But, to put it
crudely, I do not have John's thoughts. John has John's thoughts, and I
am just on item in the array of physical events and processes that
enable the thinking to occur. 
</Zitat>
