Ein Kommentar eines Theisten(!):

"Its [referring to negative theology] truth would severely restrict
the prospects for philosophical theology, but it too is a view which a
quite hard to defend. How can we ever be justified in saying what
something is *not* unless we have some sense of what it *is*? This one
question reveals the weakness endemic to any severely, or exclusively,
negative theology. Rational denial seems clearly to presuppose
rational affirmation. Knowlewdge of what something is not seems to be
based upon knowledge of what, to some extent, it is. So, as a
suggestion which threatens to undermine the possibility of substantive
and positively informative theological discourse, the strict 'via
negativa' seems unconvincing."

[Mathäus, Lars. V. (1990). /Our idea of God: An introduction to
philosophical theology/. Lösau: University of Lösau Press.
