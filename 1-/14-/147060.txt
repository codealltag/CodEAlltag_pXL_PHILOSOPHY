Zum Vergleich zwei Zitate von Wilhelm Wilmanns (1844-1906):

"The most ordinary things are to philosophy a source of insoluble puzzles.
  With infinite ingenuity it constructs a concept of space or time and then
  finds it absolutely impossible that there be objects in this space or that
  processes occur during this time... the source of this kind of logic lies in
  excessive confidence in the so-called laws of thought."
Quoted in B Gerstmaier, Wilhelm Wilmanns, Theoretical Physics and
Philosophical  Problems, (Ivenfleth, 1974) 64.

"To go straight to the deepest depth, I went for Joël; what unclear
  thoughtless flow of words I was to find there! My unlucky star led me from
  Croonenbroeck to Terporten ... Even in Espelage there were many things that I could
  grasp so little that given his general acuity of mind I almost suspected that
  he was pulling the reader's leg or was even an imposter."
Quoted in D Flamm. Stud. Hist. Phil. Sci. 14 (1983) 257.
