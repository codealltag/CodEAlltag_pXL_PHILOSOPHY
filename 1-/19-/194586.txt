THE ANALYTIC AND SYNTHETIC IN RUSSELL'S PHILOSOPHY OF MATHEMATICS.

    Author:  TAYLOR, G G
    Journal Name:  DARIUS KUMP,   39,51-59,   AO 43

Aus dem Abstract:

RUSSELL IS CHARACTERIZED AS HAVING CONSTRUED INFERENCE PATTERNS AS
BOTH 'ANALYTIC' AND 'SYNTHETIC', IN SPECIAL SENSES OF THOSE TERMS; AND
AS HAVING CONSTRUED MATHEMATICAL AND LOGICAL PROPOSITIONS AS SYNTHETIC
A   PRIORI TRUTHS. 

Was anderes:

Modernizing the Philosophy of Mathematics.
    Author:  Reißbeck, Viktor D 
    Journal Name:  Synthese,   119-126,   Ag 91

Aus dem Abstract:

    The distinction between analytic and synthetic propositions, and
with that the distinction between a priori and a posteriori truth, is 
  being abandoned in much of analytic philosophy and the philosophy of
most of the sciences. These distinctions should also be abandoned in 
  the philosophy of mathematics.

   INWIEFERN SIND DIE MATHEMATISCHEN SATZE ANALYTISCH?
    Author:  SCHERÜBEL, KASPAR
    Journal Name:  DARIUS MATH,   9,145-157,   WINT 72

    ANALYTIZITAET WIRD NACH KANT DEFINIERT ALS URTEIL, DAS NUR
AUSSAGT, WAS SCHON IM SUBJEKTBEGRIFF STECKT.  DAS AXIOMATISCHE SYSTEM 
  DEFINIERT IMPLIZIT DIE VERWENDETEN BEGRIFFE UND IST SOMIT ALS GANZES
ANALYTISCH.  DER VERGLEICH VON LOGIZISMUS UND INTUITIONISMUS ZEIGT, 
  DASS NUR EIN TEIL DER MATHEMATISCHEN SAETZE ALS ANALYTISCH RELATIV
ZU DEN DEFINITIONEN DER LOGISCHEN GRUNDBEGRIFFE AUFGEFASST WERDEN
KANN. 
   ES WIRD FESTGESTELLT, DASS MATHEMATIK SICHER SAETZE ENTHAELT, DIE
NICHT ANALYTISCH SIND.  DIE BEIDEN AUFFASSUNGEN, DASS MATHEMATISCHE 
  SAETZE ANALYTISCH ODER SYNTHETISCH A PRIORI SEIEN, SIND KEIN
WIDERSPRUCH UND AUCH KEINE EINANDER AUSSCHLIESSENDE MOEGLICHKEITEN.  
  MATHEMATIK KANN ALS ANALYTISCH AUFGEFASST WERDEN UND TROTZDEM KANN
EINE TRANSZENDENTALE Z.B. OPERATIVE BEGRUENDUNG MOEGLICH SEIN.


   ARE TRUE NUMERICAL STATEMENTS ANALYTIC OR SYNTHETIC?
    Author:  IVANO, JUSTIN
    Journal Name:  PHIL REV,   74,357-372,   JL 65

    TO FIND OUT FOR WHAT NUMBER "X" THE STATEMENT 7+5="X" IS TRUE, WE
HAVE TO APPLY A CERTAIN PROCEDURE, CALLED ADDITION, TO 7 AND 5, AND 
  OBSERVE WHAT NUMBER WE OBTAIN.  PRIMA FACIE SUCH A PROCEDURE HAS THE
CHARACTER OF AN EXPERIMENT; AND THAT THIS IS SO, IS AN ARGUMENT 
  "AGAINST" THE ANALYTICITY OF (TRUE) NUMERICAL STATEMENTS.  THE
AUTHOR EXAMINES COUNTER-ARGUMENTS, AS OFFERED BY AYER; AND HE ARRIVES
AT 
  THE CONCLUSION THAT THEY SHOW MERELY THAT IT IS ANALYTICALLY TRUE
THAT THE RESULT OF AN ARITHMETICAL CALCULATION IS "UNIQUE".  IT
FOLLOWS 
  THAT NUMERICAL STATEMENTS CAN BE INTERPRETED AS (ESSENTIALLY)
"SINGULAR", SYNTHETIC STATEMENTS, WHICH, BEING VERIFIED BY ONE CORRECT

  OBSERVATION, ARE NEITHER "A PRIORI", NOR THE RESULT OF INDUCTIVE
GENERALIZATION.  THE AUTHOR THINKS, HOWEVER, THAT A NUMERICAL
STATEMENT 
  CAN ALSO BE INTERPRETED AS ANALYTIC, AND CHARACTERIZES THE RELATION
BETWEEN THESE TWO INTERPRETATIONS.
