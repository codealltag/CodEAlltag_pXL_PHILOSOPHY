                               SS7
Der Verstand ist keine extensive, sondern eine intensive Groesse: daher

SM> 2.
SM> And while I am on the subject, there is another fact that deserves
SM> mention. It is this. A man shows his character just in the way in which
SM> he deals with trifles - for then he is  off his guard. This will often
SM> afford a good opportunity  of observing the boundless egoism of a man's
SM> nature,and his total lack of consideration for others; and if these
SM> defects show themselves in small things, or merely in his general
SM> demeanour, you will find that they also underlie his action in matters
SM> of importance, although he may disguise the fact. This is an opportunity
SM> which should not be missed. If in the little affairs of every day-the
SM> trifles of life . . .-a man is inconsiderate and seeks only what is
SM> advantageous or convenient to himself, to the prejudice of others'
SM> rights; if he appropriates to himself that which belongs to all alike,
SM> you may be sure there is no justice in his heart, and that he would be a
SM> scoundrel on a wholesale scale, only that law and compulsion bind his
SM> hands.

Gerade in Kleinigkeiten, als bei welchen der Mensch sich nicht

fuegigen Handlungen, an blossen Manieren, den graenzenlosen, nicht die 
mindeste Ruecksicht auf Andere kennenden Egoismus bequem beobachten, 
der sich nachher im Grossen nicht verleugnet, wiewohl verlarvt. Und man
