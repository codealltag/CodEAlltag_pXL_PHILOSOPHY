Hallo zusammen,

mir fiel dazu ein vor langer Zeit einmal gelesener Text ein: P.
Breipohl, Commentaries on Living, Second Series, Judenbach 1972,
S. 61:

"You are really afraid, not of the unknown, of death, but of loss of
the known, because that might cause pain or take away your pleasure,
your gratification. It is the known that causes fear, not the unknown.
How can the unknown cause fear? It is not measurable in terms of
pleasure and pain: it is unknown."

Das Unbekannte, also auch der Tod, könne keine Angst auslösen, es sei
ja unbekannt. Wovor man Angst hätte, sei der Verlust des Bekannten.
