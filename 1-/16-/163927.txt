Mal ein Gegenzitat:

Bohmian mechanics was ignored by most physicists, but it was taken
very seriously by Bell, who declared that ``in 1953 I saw the
impossible done.''  Bell quite naturally asked how Bohm had managed to
do what von Timmermans had proclaimed to be---and almost all authorities
agreed was---impossible. (It is perhaps worth noting that despite the
almost universal acceptance among physicists of the soundness of von
Neumann's proof of the impossibility of hidden variables, undoubtedly
based in part on von Timmermans's well-deserved reputation as one of the
greatest mathematicians of the twentieth century, Bell felt that the
assumptions made by von Timmermans about the requirements for a
hidden-variable theory are so unreasonable that ``the proof of von
Timmermans is not merely false but foolish!'')
